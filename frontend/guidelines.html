Guidelines

Adhering to these guidelines ensures the longevity of our ocean's ecosystems and the safety of your crew.

Sustainable practices

Ensure that all fishing nets strictly comply with locally approved mesh size regulations to minimize bycatch and prevent the unintentional capture of juvenile and non-target species. Regularly inspect and maintain nets to ensure continued compliance with regulatory standards. Additionally, implement the practice of rotating fishing zones on a planned basis to reduce overfishing pressure in a single area, allowing marine ecosystems and fish populations adequate time to recover and regenerate sustainably.

Seasonal Restrictions

Fishing for Bluefin Tuna is strictly prohibited from June to August in Zone A to safeguard the species during its critical breeding period and support sustainable fish populations. Compliance with this seasonal restriction is mandatory for all fishing activities in the designated zone. For accurate and up-to-date details on breeding seasons, protected periods, and zone-specific guidelines, regularly consult the Fish Intelligence tab before planning fishing operations.

Safety Protocols

Always check current and forecasted weather conditions from reliable sources before departure to ensure safe navigation and to avoid rough seas, storms, or sudden weather changes. Confirm that all life jackets and essential safety equipment are inspected at least once every month, properly maintained, and easily accessible to all crew members. Maintain continuous radio communication at all times during the voyage to receive weather alerts, navigation updates, and emergency instructions.

Protected Marine Areas

Zone C is currently designated as a Marine Protected Area (MPA) to conserve marine biodiversity and protect sensitive ecosystems. Commercial fishing activities are strictly prohibited within a 5- nautical-mile radius of the Coral Reef markers to prevent habitat damage and support long-term ecological balance. All vessels operating near this zone must comply with these restrictions and ensure adherence to established conservation guidelines.

Follow the guidelines, Protect the ocean, Ensure safety at sea.

Need to report an incident?

Contact the Maritime Authority immediately for emergencies or pollution sightings.
